SPORTS PREDICTION MARKETS GUIDE

GENERAL SPORTS BETTING PRINCIPLES

Home Advantage:
- NFL: home teams win ~57% of regular season games
- NBA: home teams win ~60% of regular season games
- MLB: home teams win ~54% of regular season games
- Soccer/Football: home teams win ~46%, draw ~27%, away win ~27%
- NHL: home teams win ~55% of regular season games

The importance of home advantage has declined slightly with modern travel and neutral-site venues.

KEY SPORTS PREDICTION CATEGORIES

1. AMERICAN FOOTBALL (NFL/College)
Super Bowl Futures: team quality metrics (DVOA, EPA/play), injuries to key positions
Conference Championship: use season-long efficiency metrics, not just records
Weekly games: injury report (practice designations Wed-Fri), weather for outdoor games
Key statistics: DVOA (Defense-adjusted Value Over Average) is most predictive team metric
Important: QB injuries massively affect win probabilities (20-25 percentage point swings)

2. BASKETBALL (NBA/College)
Regular season: net rating, offensive/defensive efficiency are most predictive
Playoff markets: rest advantages, home court, injury status, coaching adjustments
NBA Finals markets: historical pattern — teams with better regular season record win 62%
Key metric: Net Rating (points per 100 possessions differential)
Season win total markets: over/unders set around true talent, slight vig edge to under

3. BASEBALL (MLB)
World Series and playoff markets: pitcher matchups matter enormously
Starting pitching quality: ERA, FIP (Fielding Independent Pitching), K-BB%
Team defense: DRS (Defensive Runs Saved), OAA (Outs Above Average)
Bullpen depth: increasingly critical in playoffs, monitor usage
Key: baseball has highest variance of major sports — upsets are common

4. SOCCER/FOOTBALL
Champions League and major tournaments:
- Market capitalization (transfer fees) correlates with team quality
- ELO ratings provide good prior for match outcomes
- Head-to-head records at specific venues matter
- Key: monitor injury news to star players (can shift probability 5-10 points)
Expected Goals (xG) metric: better predictor of future results than actual goals scored

5. GOLF MAJORS (Masters, US Open, The Open, PGA Championship)
Scoring average and strokes gained metrics at similar course types
Weather conditions affect different players differently
Favorites win less often than other sports due to 72-hole format and large field
Base rate: top-ranked players win majors at rate of roughly 20-25% (when in top 5 favorites)

6. TENNIS (Grand Slams)
Surface specialization: clay specialists (Nadal on Roland Garros), grass specialists
Head-to-head records, especially at specific surfaces and stages
Current form: tournament results in preceding 2-3 weeks
Serve statistics: first serve percentage, aces, service games won

TOURNAMENT ADVANCEMENT PROBABILITY ESTIMATION

Bracket-based approach for elimination tournaments:
1. Assign win probability to each potential matchup
2. Calculate probability of each advancement scenario
3. Multiply path probabilities to get advancement probability

Example: Team A has 70% chance of winning vs Team B, and 40% chance if they face Team C
If Team B and Team C are equally likely to be opponent: 0.5*(0.70) + 0.5*(0.40) = 0.55

READING SPORTS MARKETS CORRECTLY

Signs of mispriced sports markets:
- Market hasn't updated after major injury announcement
- Market conflicts significantly with betting market odds (can arbitrage or cross-reference)
- Market price inconsistent with game-by-game probability calculations
- Low volume (<$20,000) for a major event

Cross-referencing with traditional sportsbooks:
Traditional sportsbooks (DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM) provide probabilities via their odds
Converting moneyline odds to probabilities:
- -150 favorite: 150/(150+100) = 60%
- +130 underdog: 100/(130+100) = 43.5% (note: vig means these don't sum to 100%)
- Remove vig to get implied probabilities: divide each by sum of both implied probs

INJURY REPORTING TIMELINE (NFL example):
- Wednesday: initial practice report (limited/full/DNP)
- Thursday: updated report
- Friday: final injury designation report
- Sunday morning: inactive list released 90 min before kickoff
Always check injury reports before trading sports markets close to game time.

WEATHER IMPACT ON OUTDOOR SPORTS
NFL outdoor games:
- Wind >20mph: reduces scoring, hurts passing teams, helps running teams
- Rain: turnover probability increases, passing efficiency decreases
- Cold (<20°F): reduces scoring, small but measurable effect
- Dome teams playing outdoors: slight disadvantage adjustment

Baseball:
- Wind direction matters enormously (blowing out = more runs)
- Temperature affects ball carry: warmer = more home runs
- Weather-delayed or postponed games change market mechanics
