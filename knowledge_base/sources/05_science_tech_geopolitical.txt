SCIENCE, TECHNOLOGY, AND GEOPOLITICAL PREDICTION MARKETS GUIDE

AI AND TECHNOLOGY MARKETS

AI Capability Milestones:
Polymarket frequently has markets on AI benchmarks, model releases, and capabilities.
Key considerations:
- AI capability improvements follow rough S-curves but with unpredictable breakthrough timing
- Major lab announcements (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Meta) drive these markets
- GPQA, ARC, MMLU and other benchmarks are commonly used as resolution criteria
- Scaling laws suggest continued capability improvement with compute investment
- Base rate: AI labs release major models approximately 2-4 times per year
- "GPT-5 by [date]" style markets: historically these models arrive later than expected

AI Company and Industry Events:
- Product launches follow no strict cadence; monitor lab communications closely
- Compute constraints (chip availability) affect training timelines
- Regulatory developments (EU AI Act, US executive orders) can delay deployments
- Monitor AI safety incidents as potential regulatory trigger events

PHARMACEUTICAL AND FDA MARKETS

FDA Approval Markets:
Resolution criteria: FDA grants Prescription Drug User Fee Act (PDUFA) approval by target date
PDUFA dates: announced when NDA/BLA is filed, typically 10-12 months after filing

Historical FDA approval rates by stage:
- Phase 1 → approval: ~9% (most failures in Phase 2 and 3)
- Phase 2 → Phase 3 success: ~28%
- Phase 3 success: ~58%
- NDA/BLA filing → approval: ~85%

Key signals for FDA markets:
- Advisory committee (AdCom) vote: FDA follows AdCom recommendation ~75-80% of time
- Complete Response Letter (CRL): indicates FDA wants more data before approval
- Clinical hold: regulatory pause on clinical development (negative signal)
- Priority Review: 6 month review (vs standard 10 months), suggests FDA sees urgency
- Fast Track designation: doesn't predict approval but indicates FDA interest
- Accelerated Approval: based on surrogate endpoints, can be granted more quickly
- Breakthrough Therapy designation: intensive FDA guidance, historically higher approval rate

SPACE AND SCIENCE MARKETS

Space Mission Markets:
- Rocket launch schedules historically slip 3-6 months from announced dates
- SpaceX Starship: each test flight attempts more complex objectives
- NASA Artemis program: crewed lunar missions face technical and budgetary delays
- Commercial crew missions: more reliable due to more mature technology

CLIMATE AND ENVIRONMENTAL MARKETS
- Annual temperature records: track NOAA/NASA GISS monthly global temperature data
- Arctic sea ice extent: monitor NSIDC data for summer minimums
- Extreme weather frequency: insurance industry loss data as signal

GEOPOLITICAL MARKETS

War and Conflict Markets:
IMPORTANT: These markets have serious human implications. Analyze with appropriate sensitivity.

Key signals for escalation vs de-escalation:
- Diplomatic channels: peace talks, ceasefire negotiations, mediator involvement
- Military signals: mobilization orders, weapons transfers, defensive vs offensive posture
- Economic pressure: sanctions effectiveness, energy dependencies
- International support: UN Security Council votes, coalition building
- Historical base rates: most crises do NOT escalate to direct military conflict

Russia-Ukraine conflict signals:
- Frontline position changes (map monitoring sites: DeepStateMap)
- Western military aid packages and delivery timelines
- Diplomatic communication between parties
- Economic sustainability on both sides

Middle East conflict signals:
- Ceasefire negotiation status
- Regional escalation risk (Hezbollah, Iran involvement)
- Humanitarian situation as diplomatic pressure
- US/Western diplomatic engagement level

TRADE AND ECONOMIC SANCTIONS MARKETS
- Tariff announcements: monitor US Trade Representative, White House
- Trade deal progress: watch for formal negotiating sessions, exchange of position papers
- Sanctions: US Treasury OFAC, EU Council decisions
- Note: trade policy markets can move rapidly on executive statements

INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATION DECISIONS (UN, WTO, NATO)
- UN Security Council: US, Russia, China veto power makes binding resolutions rare
- NATO decisions require consensus (all 32 members must agree for major commitments)
- WTO dispute resolution: slow process, typically takes 2-4 years for final ruling
- IMF/World Bank: loan approval markets depend on conditionality negotiations

TECHNOLOGY REGULATION MARKETS
EU Digital Markets Act (DMA) enforcement:
- EU moves slower than headlines suggest for formal enforcement actions
- Appeals processes extend timelines significantly
- Fine sizes often lower than maximum possible fines

US Antitrust actions:
- DOJ and FTC antitrust cases take 2-5 years from filing to resolution
- Settlement more likely than trial victory for government
- Market structure matters: horizontal mergers face higher scrutiny than vertical

CELEBRITY AND ENTERTAINMENT MARKETS
Award show prediction (Oscars, Grammys, etc.):
- Gold Derby consensus is most predictive single signal
- Previous awards circuit (Golden Globes, SAG, BAFTA) strongly predicts Oscars
- Historical patterns: Best Picture Oscar winner almost always wins Best Director or Best Editing
- Market correlations: films nominated in more categories win Best Picture at higher rates

Box office markets:
- Early tracking estimates released 2-3 weeks before opening
- Opening weekend is main resolution metric for most markets
- Seasonal patterns: summer and holiday weekends skew higher
- Competition analysis: direct competition from other wide releases in same weekend
